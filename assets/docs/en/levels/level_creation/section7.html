7.1 Purpose

Define how the overall look of a level is established using time of day, sky, fog/atmosphere and local lights.

7.2 Core concepts

Time of Day (tod), sun, sky and all the environmental features can be controlled through level objects included in the level_objects folder found in the Scene Tree.

Fog/atmosphere shapes depth perception and aerial perspective and can be controlled through theLevelInfo object.

Local lights provide night and area illumination.

7.3 Requirements and considerations

Choose a default time-of-day setup that preserves road and terrain readability while driving and validate visibility and contrast at multiple times of day.

Keep local light counts conservative, especially in dense urban or forested areas.

Ensure key routes remain readable at dusk and night with minimal discomfort (glare, excessive darkness).

7.4 Fog and atmosphere

Use fog to support depth cues without washing out mid‑tones.

Keep color temperature consistent with the chosen sky and terrain palette.

The main parameters to check are

  • fogDensity: change the density of the fog. Stick to lower values in order to gain the most realistic results (0.001 gives already nice results)
  • fogDensityOffset: defines the offset of the fog in meters from the camera position where it starts

7.5 Local lighting

Prefer targeted point/spot lights for points of interest and road furniture (intersections, tunnels).

Avoid heavy overlaps; set ranges and intensities suitable for driving speeds and sight distances.

There are 2 local lighting you can choose.

  1. Select Create Object
  2. Select Environment
  3. You can select Point Light or Spot Light

Creating a Point or Spot Light from the toolbar

Point Light

Point lights are omni directional lights that expands in all directions and controlled by the radius parameter.

Some other main important parameter to setup a correct lighting effect are the following

Point Light parameters in the Inspector

isEnabled: simply turn the light on or off

color: used to define the light color

brightness: controls the intensity of the light

castShadows: turn the light shadows on or off

attenuationRatio: this defines the proportions of constant, linear and quadratic attenuation of the light falloff

overDarkFactor: this is the ESM shadow darkening factor

Instead of radius spotlights are controlled by 3 parameters: range, innerAngle, outerAngle

7.6 PostFX

PostFX (post-processing effects) are applied after the scene is rendered and shape the final look of the level. They are configured through the PostFX manager and stored with the level’s environment setup.

Common effects you will tune for a level:

  • SSAO (ambient occlusion): adds contact shadowing in crevices and where objects meet. Use moderate intensity so it grounds objects without dirtying the whole scene.
  • Bloom: bleeds light from bright areas. Keep it subtle so emissive signs and the sun read well without washing out the image.
  • Depth of field / motion blur / vignette / color grading: optional stylistic effects. Use sparingly, since heavy effects can hurt readability and performance while driving.
Validate PostFX at multiple times of day. An exposure or bloom setup that looks good at noon can make dusk and night scenes unreadable.

7.7 Performance guidance

Budget the number of active dynamic lights in high‑density scenes or you’ll fill memory in no time which will cause a consistent frame rate drop.

Shadows can also have a big impact. Don’t push too much with shadows definition.

7.8 Data locations

All the data regarding sky, sun, fog, clouds, etc. are stored in their specific json files. level_objects should be the default folder containing all the lights and atmosphere specific objects.

See also: TimeOfDay , LevelInfo , Lights .

7.9 Common issues

Excessive fog or contrast can lead to “milky” or overly dark and unrealistic scenes.

Color casts should be controlled with real values or it can conflict with the terrain, vegetation and the overall look and feel of the map.

Too many local lights can cause frame drops or harsh overlaps.

7.10 Validation criteria

  • Day and night profiles remain readable while driving at speed.
  • Fog adds depth without degrading contrast.
  • PostFX and lights maintain stable performance in dressed areas.
